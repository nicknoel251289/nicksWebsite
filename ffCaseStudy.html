FitForm Case Study

It seems like every year, there is some new fitness trend taking over that is marketed as the “latest and greatest”. With the rise of technology, being sedentary has also become the new norm. Often times people with limited fitness knowledge are participating in these trends and injuring themselves because they have no basic fitness knowledge. Every day, there are more than 10,000 people treated in emergency rooms across the country for injuries stemming from exercise, sports, and recreation.

FitForm is an application I designed that utilizes both fitness and technology to bring people back to basics. Users select different exercises to perform then the application will use the smart phones camera to scan their bodies and assess their form against correct form. Depending on the results, each user will receive suggestions for corrective exercises that will help them achieve proper form. The better the form, the better the results, the less injuries there are.

My Role

As an idea envisioned myself, I was able to play all the roles of the project: client and designer. It was a lot of fun being able to fully design and create how everything would be presented.

Goals & Objective

The primary goals of the project were:

  • Design a landing page for a mobile fitness application
  • Create a palette that is energetic and professional
  • Demonstrate some of the applications features
  • Give information on how this application is beneficial

The Research

After examining results from user surveys, it’s highly suggestive that a large majority of people going to gyms doing free weight exercises are not entirely sure if they perform exercises with proper motor mechanics. The conclusion for this stems from answers pertaining to if people in the gym see other gym goers lifting with improper form and if they view themselves as lifting with proper form. Additionally as a former personal trainer, I have first hand experience working with people who think they know proper form, but in reality do not. After speaking with other personal trainers, it seems this is often the case. The surveys, as well as my own experiences, were instrumental in determining what features would be implemented into the application and website.

Personas

The personas created were designed to depict various people with various fitness levels. FitForm will be available for everyone, so it was important that no matter what their knowledge is, there would be something for them and it will be easy to use.

Iteration & Design

Because of the nature of only creating a landing page and because it was a new idea, it was important to know exactly what users would be specifically looking for. For this reason the layout was iterated multiple times to try to best deliver and highlight the areas thought to be most important. I also wanted to point out the pricing of the application as an additional highlight.

Preference Testing

Preference testing was a big part of this project. Making sure the visual features of the landing page matched well with the app. For this reason I created a style guide that could be applied to both.

The Results

The FitForm landing page demonstrates effectively all the important features that a majority of people would use or want to use out of a fitness application. In an energetic and professional style, the landing page also showcases how this application can easily be beneficial by being incorporated into someone's life from a monetary standpoint and from an overall physical well being standpoint.
